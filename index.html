1. The Problem Context

Theoretical Disconnect

Standard SCM literature, such as Chopra & Meindl (2016), emphasizes "Strategic Fit"â€”aligning supply chain responsiveness with demand uncertainty. However, traditional models fail to account for the velocity of modern data, leading to a disconnect between strategic planning (theory) and operational execution (reality).

Operational Inefficiency

Despite the prevalence of ERP systems, organizations suffer from "Information Latency." Simchi-Levi et al. (2008) highlight that without real-time visibility, the Bullwhip Effect amplifies variance across the chain, increasing inventory holding costs and reducing service levels.

2. Research Objectives

  • To Develop: A Dynamic Decision Support System (DDSS) framework that integrates disparate logistics data sources (SQL/IoT) into a unified cognitive model.
  • To Analyze: The impact of real-time Visual Analytics (Power BI/Tableau) on reducing information asymmetry in multi-echelon networks.
  • To Propose: An affordable, low-code implementation strategy for SMEs to achieve "Agility" as defined by Christopher (2000).

4. Research Methodology

The research follows the Design Science Research (DSR) paradigm as outlined by Hevner et al. (2004), suitable for creating and evaluating IT artifacts in business contexts.

The Relevance Cycle

Identifying problems within the specific context of Logistics & SCM (using Case Studies & Interviews).

The Design Cycle

Iterative development of the Framework and Software Prototype (Python/Power BI).

The Rigor Cycle

Grounding the solution in established theories: Theory of Constraints and Information Processing Theory.
