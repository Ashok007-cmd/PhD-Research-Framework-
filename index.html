Adaptive Business Intelligence for
Resilient Supply Chain Ecosystems

Bridging the gap between traditional SCM theory and real-time data analytics using Python, SQL, and Dashboarding technologies.

Researcher: Ashok Kumar V
Target Degree: PhD in SCM

The Problem Statement

Traditional Supply Chain Management (SCM) relies heavily on historical data and static forecasting methods. As highlighted in your reference texts (scintro_s.pdf), obstacles to achieving "Strategic Fit" include information coordination issues and local optimization rather than global optimization.

Despite the abundance of data (ERP, IoT), organizations struggle to convert this into actionable, real-time intelligence. There is a disconnect between strategic planning (MBA theory) and operational data execution (BI Analysis).

Research Objectives

  • To develop a Dynamic Decision Support System (DDSS) framework that integrates disparate logistics data sources (SQL/SAP).
  • To quantify the impact of real-time visualization (Power BI/Tableau) on reducing the Bullwhip Effect in multi-echelon chains.
  • To propose a predictive model using Python-based Machine Learning for demand sensing in volatile markets (e.g., FMCG/Logistics).

Identifying the Research Gaps

The Latency Gap

Literature: Discusses "Information Sharing" (Ref: 3SupplyChainManagement.pdf).
Reality: Data exists but reporting is retrospective.
Contribution: Moving from Descriptive Analytics to Prescriptive Analytics.

The Technical-Managerial Gap

Literature: Focuses on management strategy or pure CS algorithms.
Reality: Managers lack tools to interpret complex algorithms.
Contribution: Creating "Human-in-the-loop" visualization dashboards (UI/UX in SCM).

The SME Adoption Gap

Literature: Enterprise-level solutions (SAP) are discussed.
Reality: SMEs lack affordable BI implementation frameworks.
Contribution: Low-code/No-code automation strategies for logistics SMEs.

Proposed Methodology

1

Mixed Methods Research

Combining quantitative data analysis (analyzing historical sales logs) with qualitative case studies (interviewing logistics managers about dashboard usability).

2

Design Science Research (DSR)

This is critical for your profile. You will not just "study" a phenomenon; you will build an artifact (a Python/Power BI prototype) and test its utility in a real-world scenario.

3

Simulation

Using Python to simulate supply chain disruptions and measuring how quickly your proposed BI alerts allow for corrective action compared to traditional methods.
